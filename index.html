Unresolved Historical Mysteries * **The "Co-cha-tubbee" Notation:** Found in Land Patent 19B (1857) for John Spencer Horner's property, this notation remains an **unexplained research question**. It is unclear if it refers to a prior land claim, a person, or a specific geographic feature. * **The Reality of the "Hidden Gold":** While the search for buried gold and silver is cited as the primary motive for the attack, its existence is **unverified by any official probate or estate records**. * **The Details of John Spencer Horner's Death:** While it is known he died in 1864, there is an open question regarding the exact sequence—specifically **whether he died instantly** during the initial confrontation on August 2 or if he survived until August 10 alongside his son. ### 2. Gaps in Official Documentation * **Verification of the Reprisal Body Count:** There is **no mention in any known military tribunal, provost marshal record, or government report** of the specific "17 out of 20" body count claimed by the Horner family. * **Primary Proof of Suspects at the Scene:** Although family tradition firmly names **Henry Stewart** and **James "Jess" Wilson** as attackers, no contemporaneous primary documentation has been found that explicitly places them at the Horner homestead during the August 1864 murders. * **Identities of the Remaining Reprisal Squad:** Beyond Dr. John Turner Horner, Wash Middleton, and Fate Arnold, the **names of the other participants** in the irregular reprisal group are not recorded. ### 3. Conflicting Records and Data * **John Turner Horner’s Military Rank:** A significant contradiction exists between family tradition, which identifies him as a **2nd Lieutenant**, and the War Department, which **rejected his pension claim** in 1894 after determining he was a recruiter who never formally mustered. * **Hiram Boen’s AWOL Justification:** While records show the War Department retroactively cleared his desertion charge in 1866, the **specific justification or Special Order** (referenced as B.O. 276-1866) explaining why he was absent during the murders has not been located. * **Identity of John Fry Hill’s Wife:** There is a discrepancy between genealogical records naming her **Hannah E. Bradshaw** and the 1860 Federal Census, which lists her as "**Emeline Hill**". ### 4. Underused Documentary Details The sources also note that certain historical anchors are often overlooked in the broader narrative of the murders, including: * **The 1752 Fairfax Land Grant:** This document provides the earliest link in the family chain, connecting the Horners to **George Washington’s surveys** in colonial Virginia. * **Professional History:** An 1836 legal notice documents a **law partnership dissolution** in Helena, Arkansas, establishing the family's professional presence in the state decades before the Civil War. * **Indigenous Citizenship Litigation:** A 1904 court card shows the family was **denied Choctaw citizenship**, which serves as a primary-source refutation of the family’s oral claims of Cherokee ancestry.
